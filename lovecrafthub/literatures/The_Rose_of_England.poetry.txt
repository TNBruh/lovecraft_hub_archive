At morn the rosebud greets the sun<br>
&emsp;And sheds the evening dew,<br>
Expanding ere the day is done,<br>
&emsp;In bloom of radiant hue;<br>
And when the sun his rest hath found,<br/>
Rose-petals strow the garden round!<br/><br/>

Thus that blest Isle that owns the Rose<br/>
&emsp;From mist and darkness came,<br/>
A million glories to disclose,<br/>
&emsp;And spread BRITANNIA’S name;<br/>
And ere Life’s Sun shall leave the blue,<br/>
ENGLAND shall reign the whole world thro’!
