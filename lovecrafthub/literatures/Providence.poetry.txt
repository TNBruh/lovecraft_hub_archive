<center><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0"><tr><td><div>

Where bay and river tranquil blend,<br>
&emsp;And leafy hillsides rise,<br>
The spires of Providence ascend<br>
&emsp;Against the ancient skies.<br><br/>

Here centuried domes of shining gold<br/>
&emsp;Salute the morning’s glare,<br/>
While slanting gables, odd and old,<br/>
&emsp;Are scatter’d here and there.<br/><br/>

And in the narrow winding ways<br/>
&emsp;That climb o’er slope and crest,<br/>
The magic of forgotten days<br/>
&emsp;May still be found to rest.<br/><br/>

A fanlight’s gleam, a knocker’s blow,<br/>
&emsp;A glimpse of Georgian brick—<br/>
The sights and sounds of long ago<br/>
&emsp;Where fancies cluster thick.<br/><br/>

A flight of steps with iron rail,<br/>
&emsp;A belfry looming tall,<br/>
A slender steeple, carv’d and pale,<br/>
&emsp;A moss-grown garden wall.<br/><br/>

A hidden churchyard’s crumbling proofs<br/>
&emsp;Of man’s mortality,<br/>
A rotting wharf where gambrel roofs<br/>
&emsp;Keep watch above the sea.<br/><br/>

Square and parade, whose walls have tower’d<br/>
&emsp;Full fifteen decades long<br/>
By cobbled ways ’mid trees embower’d,<br/>
&emsp;And slighted by the throng.<br/><br/>

Stone bridges spanning languid streams,<br/>
&emsp;Houses perch’d on the hill,<br/>
And courts where mysteries and dreams<br/>
&emsp;The brooding spirit fill.<br/><br/>

Steep alley steps by vines conceal’d,<br/>
&emsp;Where small-pan’d windows glow<br/>
At twilight on a bit of field<br/>
&emsp;That chance has left below.<br/><br/>

My Providence!  What airy hosts<br/>
&emsp;Turn still thy gilded vanes;<br/>
What winds of elf that with grey ghosts<br/>
&emsp;People thine ancient lanes!<br/><br/>

The chimes of evening as of old<br/>
&emsp;Above thy valleys sound,<br/>
While thy stern fathers ’neath the mould<br/>
&emsp;Make blest thy sacred ground.<br/><br/>

Thou dream’st beside the waters there,<br/>
&emsp;Unchang’d by cruel years;<br/>
A spirit from an age more fair<br/>
&emsp;That shines behind our tears.<br/><br/>

Thy twinkling lights each night I see,<br/>
&emsp;Tho’ time and space divide;<br/>
For thou art of the soul of me,<br/>
&emsp;And always at my side!
