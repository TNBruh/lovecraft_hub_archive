<center><b>Revelation<br/>By H. P. Lovecraft</b></center><br/><div><div align="justify">
<center><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0"><tr><td><div>

In a vale of light and laughter,<br>
&emsp;Shining ’neath the friendly sun,<br>
Where fulfilment follow’d after<br>
&emsp;Ev’ry hope or dream begun;<br>
Where an Aidenn gay and glorious,<br/>
&emsp;Beckon’d down the winsome way;<br/>
There my soul, o’er pain victorious,<br/>
&emsp;Laugh’d and lingered—yesterday.<br/><br/>

Green and narrow was my valley,<br/>
&emsp;Temper’d with a verdant shade;<br/>
Sun-deck’d brooklets musically<br/>
&emsp;Sparkled thro’ each glorious glade;<br/>
And at night the stars serenely<br/>
&emsp;Glow’d betwixt the boughs o’erhead,<br/>
While Astarte, calm and queenly,<br/>
&emsp;Floods of fairy radiance shed.<br/><br/>

There amid the tinted bowers,<br/>
&emsp;Raptur’d with the opiate spell<br/>
Of the grasses, ferns, and flowers,<br/>
&emsp;Poppy, phlox and pimpernel,<br/>
Long I lay, entranc’d and dreaming,<br/>
&emsp;Pleas’d with Nature’s bounteous store,<br/>
Till I mark’d the shaded gleaming<br/>
&emsp;Of the sky, and yearn’d for more.<br/><br/>

Eagerly the branches tearing,<br/>
&emsp;Clear’d I all the space above,<br/>
Till the bolder gaze, high faring,<br/>
&emsp;Scann’d the naked skies of Jove;<br/>
Deeps unguess’d now shone before me,<br/>
&emsp;Splendid beam’d the solar car;<br/>
Wings of fervid fancy bore me<br/>
&emsp;Out beyond the farthest star.<br/><br/>

Reaching, gasping, wishing, longing<br/>
&emsp;For the pageant brought to sight,<br/>
Vain I watch’d the gold orbs thronging<br/>
&emsp;Round celestial poles of light.<br/>
Madly on a moonbeam ladder<br/>
&emsp;Heav’n’s abyss I sought to scale,<br/>
Ever wiser, ever sadder,<br/>
&emsp;As the fruitless task would fail.<br/><br/>

Then, with futile striving sated,<br/>
&emsp;Veer’d my soul to earth again,<br/>
Well content that I was fated<br/>
&emsp;For a fair, yet low domain;<br/>
Pleasing thoughts of glad tomorrows,<br/>
&emsp;Like the blissful moments past,<br/>
Lull’d to rest my transient sorrows,<br/>
&emsp;Still’d my godless greed at last.<br/><br/>

But my downward glance, returning,<br/>
&emsp;Shrank in fright from what it spy’d;<br/>
Slopes in hideous torment burning,<br/>
&emsp;Terror in the brooklet’s tide:<br/>
For the dell, of shade denuded<br/>
&emsp;By my desecrating hand,<br/>
’Neath the bare sky blaz’d and brooded<br/>
&emsp;As a lost, accursed land.<br/>
