&emsp;There is snow on the ground,<br>
&emsp;&emsp;And the valleys are cold,<br>
&emsp;And a midnight profound<br>
&emsp;&emsp;Blackly squats o’er the wold;<br>
But a light on the hilltops half-seen hints of feastings unhallow’d and old.<br/><br/>
&emsp;There is death in the clouds,<br/>
&emsp;&emsp;There is fear in the night,<br/>
&emsp;For the dead in their shrouds<br/>
&emsp;&emsp;Hail the sun’s turning flight,<br/>
And chant wild in the woods as they dance round a Yule-altar fungous and white.<br/><br/>
&emsp;To no gale of earth’s kind<br/>
&emsp;&emsp;Sways the forest of oak,<br/>
&emsp;Where the sick boughs entwin’d<br/>
&emsp;&emsp;By mad mistletoes choke,<br/>
For these pow’rs are the pow’rs of the dark, from the graves of the lost Druid-folk.<br/><br/>
&emsp;And mayst thou to such deeds<br/>
&emsp;&emsp;Be an abbot and priest,<br/>
&emsp;Singing cannibal greeds<br/>
&emsp;&emsp;At each devil-wrought feast,<br/>
And to all the incredulous world shewing dimly the sign of the beast.
